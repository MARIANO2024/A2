Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Firstly, it was really useful to examine the population data in its simplest form (as a frequency diagram) as it allowed me to quickly spot major errors in the data set regarding the census part of the data. As far as the exploratory process, it was useful to create Calculated Fields for ratios rather than compare raw numbers. Especially beacuse the dataset's population is heavily skewed towards Boston, so not talking in terms of percents or ratios would have probably made every visualization seem Boston-centric. As far as trends that can be observed from this data set and the figures above, it is clear that the areas with single family only zoning are dominated by wealthy, white, young famlilies (young meaning they have a high ratio of Under 18 population).
